# Tax return folder structure (Canton of St. Gallen)

For your tax return in the Canton of St. Gallen, it pays off to use a clear folder structure that repeats every year so you can find everything quickly—both digitally and on paper.

**Key principle:** separate by tax year and group documents logically within each year.

## Recommended top-level structure

```text
└── 2025/
└── 2024/
└── 2023/
```

## Structure inside a tax year (e.g., 2025)

```text
2025/
├── 01_Personal
├── 02_Income
├── 03_Assets
├── 04_Deductions
├── 05_Debts
├── 06_Real_Estate (if applicable)
```

## Contents of each folder

### `01_Personal`

- AHV/AVS (social security) card
- Civil status documents
- Children (education confirmations, etc.)

### `02_Income`

- Salary certificate(s)
- Additional income
- Daily allowances (unemployment insurance, sick pay)
- Pensions (AHV/IV / pension fund)

### `03_Assets`

- Bank account statements as of 31.12.
- Securities statement (broker/depot)
- Cryptocurrencies
- Life insurance (cash surrender value)

### `04_Deductions` (very important!)

Subfolders are especially helpful here:

```text
04_Deductions/
├── Work_Expenses
├── Continuing_Education
├── Medical_Expenses
├── Pillar_3a
└── Childcare
```

Typical receipts:

- Public transport passes / commuting costs
- Doctor/dentist invoices
- Donation confirmations
- Pillar 3a contribution confirmation

### `05_Debts`

- Mortgages
- Loans
- Interest statements

### `06_Real_Estate` (if applicable)

- Imputed rental value
- Maintenance costs
- Renovation invoices
